Pause

Freeze billing for a set time. They come back when you say.

01
Use when
A family says "we're going on vacation for 3 weeks" or "we need a break for the holidays."
You click
Pick a start date and end date
→
Stripe
Next charge moves out by the pause length
→
Roster
Status flips to PAUSED
For the parent
  • Stops getting charged immediately
  • Card stays on file
  • Next charge happens on the resume date
In your roster
  • Member moves to the Paused tab
  • A "pause" row gets logged with start + end dates
  • Resume date is visible on their card
🟢 Auto-magic
When the resume date hits and Stripe successfully charges them, the portal automatically flips status back to LIVE. You don't have to do anything.

How the resume date is calculated
The formula
next_charge + pause_length
Pause length = end_date − start_date
Example
Feb 5 + 7d = Feb 12
Cycle to Feb 5, pause Jan 15 → Jan 22 (7d), next charge Feb 12
Stripe cap
max 730 days
Resume date is capped if you pick further out.
Edge cases — the stuff to know
01
Pause always extends the next charge by exactly the pause length. Whether the pause is 1 day or 4 weeks, the next charge date moves out by exactly that much. No surprises. The parent's unused paid time isn't lost — it gets folded into the extension.

Example
Parent paid $59 on Jan 5 (cycle Jan 5 → Feb 5).
Today is Jan 15. You pause: start Jan 15, end Jan 22 (7 days).
Next charge moves: Feb 5 + 7 days = Feb 12.
On Feb 12, Stripe charges them $59 for the next cycle.
Net: pause length is preserved as a billing extension.
02
End date is capped at ~730 days from today. Stripe's hard limit. If you pick an end date further out, the system clamps it and the response tells you. For permanent pauses, use Cancel instead.
03
Their card has to still work on resume day. If their card expires during the pause and you don't get it updated, the resume-day charge will fail → status flips to PAYMENT FAILED. Solution: send them the Payment Link a few days before the resume date if you're worried.
04
You can shift the pause without unpausing. Pause too short? Click Unpause and pick a "new resume date" — Stripe just shifts the resume date out. The pause row in your roster updates to the new end date. No extra charges either way.
05
Plan changes during pause work fine. You can use Change Plan on a paused member. The new price just kicks in when they resume — no immediate charge, no proration.
06
Pause amount = whatever their plan currently is. The resume-day charge is whatever the member's current plan costs. If you changed their plan during the pause, the new amount applies. If you didn't, it's the same amount they were paying before.

Unpause

Resume billing — either now, or push the pause to a new date.

02
Use when
They came back early OR they want to extend the pause to a different date.
You click
Pick resume now or set a new resume date
→
Stripe
Charges immediately (resume now) or shifts the future charge date
→
Roster
Flips to LIVE or stays PAUSED with new date
For the parent
  • Resume now → next charge is today
  • New date → next charge moves to that date
In your roster
  • The open pause row gets closed (or shifted)
  • Member returns to your Live tab

Cancel

End the membership. Two flavors: at end of period, or right now.

03
Use when
The family is quitting the academy. Default = let them finish what they already paid for.
You click
Pick at end of period (default) or cancel now
→
Stripe
Sub flagged to end at period close or killed immediately
→
Roster
CANCELLING (stays on roster) or removed instantly
For the parent
  • End of period: they finish their paid month, no more charges after
  • Immediate: billing stops today (no refund of the partial month)
In your roster
  • End of period: stays on roster with amber CANCELLING pill, auto-removed when their month ends
  • Immediate: gone from the roster right away
  • A "cancel" row is logged either way
🟢 Auto-magic
If a member is "Cancelling," the system waits for Stripe to officially end the sub at period close, then removes them. You'll never see a "ghost" charge against a cancelled member.
↺

Refund

Give money back for a specific charge. Full or partial.

04
Use when
They paid for something they shouldn't have — duplicate charge, billed in error, agreed-on goodwill refund.
You click
Pick the charge + amount (default: full)
→
Stripe
Sends the money back to their original card
→
Roster
Refund row logged. Membership not affected.
For the parent
  • Money lands back on their card in 3–5 business days
  • Membership keeps going as normal
In your roster
  • A refund row is logged with the amount, date, and reason
  • Member status doesn't change
🟢 Auto-magic
Even if a refund happens directly in the Stripe Dashboard (not through this button), the portal still catches it and adds it to the refund log. Your records stay complete.

Change Plan

Move them between plans: 1/wk, 2/wk, 3/wk, or unlimited.

05
Use when
A family wants to come more often (upgrade) or scale back (downgrade).
You click
Pick the new plan + (upgrade only) yes/no on prorating
→
Stripe
Updates the sub price. Optional: charge the prorated difference now.
→
Roster
Plan label updates instantly
For the parent
  • Upgrade + prorate: charged the price difference now, new amount from next cycle
  • Upgrade no prorate: new amount starts next cycle only
  • Downgrade: no refund — new lower amount starts next cycle
In your roster
  • Plan pill updates (e.g. 1/wk → 2/wk)
  • Audit log records the old and new plan
⚠ Watch out for
"Prorate now" only works on upgrades. Downgrades never charge or refund a partial amount — Stripe just bills the new lower price next cycle.

Payment Link

A self-service link so the parent can update their card.

06
Use when
Their card got declined, expired, or they want to switch to a new card. Saves you the back-and-forth.
You click
Button creates a personalized link
→
Auto
Link is copied to your clipboard + opens in a new tab to preview
→
You
Paste it into text/email to the parent
For the parent
  • Lands on Stripe's hosted page (looks branded, safe)
  • Can update card, view invoices, change billing email
  • Can self-cancel (if you've enabled that in Stripe)
In your roster
  • Card update gets logged ("card updated · Visa ····1234")
  • If they were PAYMENT FAILED, next successful charge auto-flips them back to LIVE
🟢 Auto-magic
You don't have to manually clear the "Payment Failed" flag. Once the parent fixes their card and Stripe retries successfully, the portal handles the rest.
⚠ Watch out for
This button only works if the member has at least one previous successful charge in Stripe (i.e. they've been billed before). Brand-new members who haven't paid yet need a different intake link.
🎁

Referred

Credit them with free weeks for sending you new families.

07
Use when
A current family brought in 1+ new paying families. Each referral = +4 weeks of free time.
You click
Enter how many people they referred (1–10)
→
Stripe
Pushes their next charge date out by 4 weeks × count
→
Roster
Referral row logged with new charge date
For the parent
  • Next charge moves out by 4 weeks per referral
  • 2 referrals = 8 free weeks, 10 referrals = 40 free weeks
  • Effectively free training during that window
In your roster
  • A referral row is logged (count, weeks added, new date)
  • Member status stays LIVE
⚠ Watch out for
Stripe has a hard 730-day max on free-time extensions. If a single referral burst would push them past 2 years, the portal caps it at 730 days and tells you.
