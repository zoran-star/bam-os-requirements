The Confirm Agent

The second sales texting agent. The booking agent gets a lead to book a free trial. The confirm agent makes sure they actually show up — and if they can't, it hands them back to the booking agent to rebook.

What it is When it fires What it does Who's in control How you train it Training flow Facts vs behavior What's live
In one sentence

When a lead is sitting in the Scheduled Trial stage of your pipeline, this agent texts them to confirm they're coming, tells them how to get there, and on "I can't make it" it hands them off — never argues, never rebooks itself.

The handoff loop: if a Scheduled-Trial lead says they can't make it, the confirm agent drops a note and bounces them back to "Responded" — the booking agent picks them up already knowing the whole story and rebooks them.

Each agent owns one stage. A lead is only ever in one stage at a time, so the two agents never talk over each other.

02

When it fires

It reaches out FIRST (proactive)

Once a trial is booked and the date is within 3 days, it sends one friendly "still good for Saturday?" text. (Reaching out the second they book — while the booking chat is still warm — would be annoying, so it waits.)

It replies when THEY text (reactive)

Any time a booked lead messages — a question, a "can we move it?", a "we'll be there" — it drafts the right response: logistics, a handoff, or a simple confirm.

The rhythm

EVERY 15 MIN
A background check looks for booked leads who need a nudge or a reply.
8AM – 9:30PM
Quiet hours respected — it never texts a parent late at night. After-hours messages wait for morning.
THEY REPLY
Any pending nudge is dropped and it re-reads what they just said.
03

What it actually does

Its three jobs

✅

Confirm they're coming

References their actual booked day & time (pulled from the calendar, never made up) and gets a clear yes.

🗺️

Help them get there

Address, directions, what to bring/wear, drop-off rules, which session — all pulled straight from your academy's info. It removes every reason a no-show happens.

↩️

Hand off if they can't make it

It does not try to rebook or sell. It captures why they can't come and passes them back to the booking agent with full context.

So every reply becomes one of three cards for you

Confirm

A normal confirmation or logistics text. You approve it (or it auto-sends if you trust it — see modes).

Handoff

"Can't make it." On your ✓ it writes the note + sends them back to the booking agent. Always needs you.

Lost

They don't want the trial at all anymore. On your ✓ it closes them out. Always needs you.

04

Who's in control (the dial)

The confirm agent has its own on/off dial, separate from the booking agent. Turning on booking does not turn this on.

Off DEFAULT

Silent. Nothing drafts, nothing sends. This is where it starts for every academy.

Hawkeye

It drafts every message and waits. You approve, edit, or skip each one before it sends. This is how you train it safely.

Self-drive

Confident confirmations send themselves. Anything unsure — and always handoffs & lost — still waits for you.

🛡️
Safety by design. Handoffs and "mark lost" never happen on their own — a human always confirms. And the booking agent was left completely untouched, so switching this on can't change how booking behaves.
05

How you train it — 3 levers

You shape the agent in plain English. No code. Three ways to teach it, from lightest to deepest:

1

Teach a lesson

A one-line correction. It overrides everything else whenever it applies. Best for "stop doing X" / "always do Y."

"Don't say 'see you soon' — always name the actual day and time."
2

Approve an example

Show it a perfect reply. It copies the tone, length and style of examples you bless.

Lead: "can I just drop her off?"
You: "Totally — drop-off's fine as long as you booked it. See you Monday at 7?"
3

Edit the brain

Open the actual sections (role, how it confirms, how it hands off…) and reword them. The deepest change — rewrites its standing instructions.

Brain → "Help them get there" → add: "Mention parking is free in the back lot."
🧪
Always test first. The Sandbox lets you chat as a fake lead and watch the agent reply — instantly, without texting anyone real. Tweak → test → tweak until it sounds like you, then let it loose in Hawkeye.
06

The end-to-end training loop

This is the full circle — how a change in your head becomes the live agent's behavior, and how you keep tightening it.

1

Open the Sandbox & play the lead

Type messages like a real parent would. Watch how the agent answers. Spot anything off — too pushy, wrong tone, missed a logistic.

▸ Train Agent → Sandbox
2

Correct it — pick a lever

Quick fix → teach a lesson. Nail the voice → approve an example. Big rule change → edit the brain.

▸ Train Agent → teach / save example / Brain editor
3

It saves to the agent's memory

Your lesson / example / brain edit is stored against your academy. It instantly becomes part of the agent's instructions — for the sandbox and the live agent.

▸ stored per-academy, no deploy needed
4

Re-test until it's right

Back to the sandbox. Same scenario, see the improvement. Loop steps 1–3 until you'd be happy sending it yourself.

▸ tweak → test → repeat
5

Flip it on in Hawkeye

Now it drafts for real booked leads — but every message lands in your inbox first. You approve / edit / skip. Each edit is a free training signal.

▸ mode = Hawkeye → Inbox approvals
6

Graduate to Self-drive (optional)

Once it's consistently right, let confident confirmations send themselves. You still see — and approve — every handoff and lost.

▸ mode = Self-drive
07

The golden rule: facts live once

Facts (your academy)

Address, schedule, prices, what-to-bring, policies. Set once in your academy info.

  • Edit your address in one place
  • Both agents instantly use the new one
  • Never gets out of sync
Behavior (how it acts)

Tone, how it confirms, how it hands off. This is the part you train.

  • Shared style across academies
  • Contains no hard facts — it points at your info
  • Your lessons & examples live here

👉 Want to fix a wrong address or time? That's a fact — change it in your academy info, not by training the agent. Want it to sound warmer or stop nagging? That's behavior — train it.
