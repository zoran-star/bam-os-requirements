Key Insights

  • Nearly equal split: Website (49%) and Systems (44%) requests have nearly equal distribution—your intake form should differentiate these paths.
  • 40% are red alerts: Two in five tickets are flagged urgent. Current single-path flow doesn't prioritize or expedite them.
  • Recurring patterns: Multiple tickets mention onboarding, automations, landing pages, and email flows—these could benefit from templated questions.
  • Freeform inefficiency: Current form asks "What's your request?" in one text area. Teams then have to parse and categorize—this should happen during intake.
  • Missing context: No indication in form whether request is blocking operations or cosmetic. Red alerts should be flagged upfront.
1
Split Intake by Category First
Before showing the detail form, ask: "Is this for your Website or Systems?" This drives different information needs.
Faster triage Right team assigned immediately
  • Website: Landing pages, VSL, copywriting, design, email templates, landing page flows
  • Systems: GHL automations, booking flows, integrations, onboarding sequences, payment flows
2
Add Red Alert Flag as Second Question
After category, ask: "Is this blocking your business or impacting customers right now?" This triggers 24-hour SLA vs. 2-day SLA.
SLA clarity Prevents wrong priorities
  • Red alerts get fast-track routing and earlier review windows
  • Standard tickets get scheduled review (e.g., twice-weekly)
  • Clients know their urgency tier upfront
3
Replace Generic Textarea with Templated Questions
Instead of "Describe what you need," ask structured questions that vary by category. Examples:
Less back-and-forth Better builds

  • Who approves final version?
