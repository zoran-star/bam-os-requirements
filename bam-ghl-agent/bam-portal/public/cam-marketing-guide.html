Every ticket now tells you who it's for, what the offer is, how urgent it is, and when it's due — so you stop chasing Mike and Google Drive. Here's the 2-minute tour.

01 · Priority

Every ticket is now tagged High or Normal

When a client submits a request, they can flag it urgent. Urgent requests show up as High priority; everything else is Normal. Your board is sorted urgent-first by default, so the top of the list is always what to do next.

02 · Deadlines

Each ticket gets an automatic due date

No more guessing turnaround. The board calculates a deadline from the priority and counts it down in business days. When it's past due, it turns red.

05 · Ownership & alerts

Every ticket has an owner, and nobody gets forgotten

  • Each ticket is auto-assigned to the client's SM (their manager) — shown right on the card.
  • When a new request lands, you get a heads-up so you're never digging to find what's new.
  • When you hit Mark Completed, the client is notified automatically in their Slack channel — no manual "hey it's done" messages. (Direct-message pings to you / the SM switch on once the Slack app scope is added.)
🔔
Heads up: the Slack DM pings turn on once your Slack is linked to the portal. Everything else above already works today. Ping Zoran to flip that on.
The flow

Start to finish

Client adds content
new campaign or add to an existing one → Content team produces the finals → Sent to your board
sorted by priority → Open it → full brief, build the ad → Post it on Meta → Mark Completed
↔
Either team can pause for the client: both content and marketing can send a Request Client Action at any point — the client gets prompted and the ticket waits on them.
⚡
The one habit: work top-down. The board already put the most urgent, closest-to-deadline tickets first — so just start at the top.
Guide cards

The content guide cards

Each offer type has a guide card — a mini brief the content team keeps: its purpose, filming tips, an example script, and example assets. When a client adds content, they see the matching card so they know exactly what to film and send. The content team owns and fills these.
