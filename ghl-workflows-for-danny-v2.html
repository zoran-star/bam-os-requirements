GHL Workflow Map — For Danny

Active automations in GoHighLevel that BAM currently runs for By Any Means GTA.
This document covers the ONBOARDING and SALES folders — the two we actively use and need to decide on for Full Control.

Each automation below includes a plain-English explanation of its purpose and every single step. Click any row to expand the full breakdown. You do not need to know GHL to understand these — every piece of jargon is explained in context. The goal is for you to see exactly what the business needs the software to do, so you can decide what to build in Full Control and what to leave in GHL.
Trigger (the event that kicks off the automation)
Action (something the system does: send a text, tag a person, etc.)
Response handler (if the lead texts back, stop the automated sequence)
Escalation (what happens if nobody responds)
Outcome / pipeline move (where the lead ends up in the sales tracker)

Key GHL Primitives These Workflows Depend On

These are the building blocks that GHL provides out of the box. To replace GHL, Full Control would need to implement all of these:

  • SMS sending — most workflows send text messages as the primary outreach channel (via Twilio or similar under the hood)
  • Email sending — secondary channel, used heavily in onboarding and long-term nurture
  • Reply detection — all "responded to..." circuit breakers need to detect when someone texts/emails back in response to a specific workflow's messages
  • Delay/wait steps — timed pauses between messages (2 minutes, 1 day, 3 weeks, 6 months — huge range)
  • Contact tagging — add/remove labels on contacts to categorize them and trigger other workflows (the glue that connects everything)
  • Pipeline stage moves — move contacts through sales pipeline stages (Interested, Responded, Scheduled Trial, Done Trial, Won, Lost)
  • Calendar/appointment triggers — fire when someone books, completes, or no-shows an appointment
  • Form submission triggers — fire when specific forms are filled out on the website
  • Payment event triggers — fire on successful or failed payments
  • Conditional branching — if/else logic based on tags, contact fields, day of week, or time
  • Remove from workflow — stop a contact's position in another running automation (critical for the circuit breaker pattern)
  • Internal notifications — send staff alerts via SMS, email, or in-app notification
