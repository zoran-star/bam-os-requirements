Section 05

The Pitch Structure — 8 Minutes

Every pitch follows this arc. It's a story, not a feature list. The goal: make them feel the problem, then show them the future.

Open — 60 sec

"I ran a basketball academy..."

Personal story. I used 6 tools. I was the marketer, accountant, scheduler, coach, and customer service rep. It was chaos. The tools didn't talk to each other. No tool told me what to do — I had to figure it out myself every single day.

Problem — 90 sec

"Every sports business is like this."

Zoom out. This isn't just me. There are hundreds of thousands of sports and fitness businesses running on duct tape. Mindbody is bloated. GHL is confusing. Nobody has built an intelligent, unified system for these operators.

Solution — 120 sec

"So we built FullControl."

Live demo. Show Sage greeting you. Show the command center. Show a member 360° view. Show the Suggest → Confirm → Execute flow. Let the product speak — it's visually stunning and radically simple.

Why Now — 60 sec

"AI changes everything for SMBs."

AI is the unlock. For the first time, software can be proactive instead of reactive. A small business owner can have a strategic advisor embedded in their operating system. This wasn't possible two years ago.

Market — 60 sec

$4B+ and fragmented.

Sports & fitness SaaS is massive and growing. Basketball first, then all sports training, then fitness, then broader service businesses. Classic vertical wedge — start narrow, expand with the same product.

Traction — 45 sec

Prototype live. LOIs in hand.

Interactive prototype deployed. UX fully designed. Business requirements mapped across 7 domains. Early conversations with academy owners showing strong intent. My own network = first 20 customers.

Team — 45 sec

"Waterloo math. Deloitte systems. Operator grit."

I'm the rare founder who's both the builder AND the customer. Analytical training + enterprise consulting + real operator experience. I've already built the product vision from first principles.

Ask — 30 sec

"Raising $X to get to 50 paying customers."

Clear use of funds: finish MVP, onboard first 50 customers, prove retention and expansion revenue. Milestone-based — not burning cash on experiments.

Section 08

Objections I'll Hear — And How I Answer Them

❓ "Isn't this just GoHighLevel for gyms?"

GHL is a horizontal platform that's notoriously complex. FullControl is AI-native, vertical-first, and radically simpler. It's the difference between Salesforce and Toast — same category, completely different product philosophy.

❓ "The market is too niche."

Basketball is the wedge, not the ceiling. Sports training → fitness → service businesses. Same playbook as Toast (restaurants → food service) or ServiceTitan (HVAC → all trades). Start narrow, expand with the platform.

❓ "Why can't incumbents just add AI?"

Bolting AI onto a bloated legacy platform creates a worse experience, not a better one. FullControl is AI-native from day one — the architecture, UX, and data model are all built around intelligence. You can't retrofit that.

❓ "Solo founder risk?"

Valid concern. I address it by (a) showing the depth of product work already done — 7 domains, full UX system, working prototype, (b) leveraging AI development tools to move at 10x speed, and (c) being actively recruiting a technical co-founder.
