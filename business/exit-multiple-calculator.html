How the math works

Step 1 — Base multiple from growth rate. Growth is the single biggest determinant of SaaS valuation. Piecewise mapping: <20% → 3–5x, 20–40% → 5–7x, 40–80% → 7–10x, >80% → 10–12x.

Step 2 — Add AI-native premium. A flat additive bump on top of the base, sourced from vertical AI M&A data showing +2–5x above baseline for AI-native architectures.

Step 3 — Apply strategic-fit multiplier. Strategic buyers pay 0–40% more than financial buyers for tight capability-gap fills.

Step 4 — Revenue-quality adjustments. NRR above 100% adds, below 100% subtracts. GRR below 90% compresses. Churn above 8% compresses.

Step 5 — Risk and diligence compressors. Architecture, IP cleanliness, data flywheel, capability fit, customer count, founder expertise, and market timing each apply a bounded multiplier. IP ambiguity is the biggest single compressor (can remove 50% of value).

Step 6 — Process uplift. Competitive tension adds 8% per additional bidder beyond the first.

Output is presented with a ±20% range because no real deal closes at the modeled number — it's a negotiation, not a formula.
