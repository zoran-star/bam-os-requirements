$27.8B — Sports Training Market

What this number represents

The total global market size for sports training services as of 2023. This includes all revenue from sports coaching, academies, camps, personal training, and youth development programs worldwide.

Where it comes from

Report: "Sports Training Market Size, Share, Trends | Growth 2035" (Published August 2024)
Key finding: $27.8B in 2023, projected to reach $50.7B by 2035, growing at 5.2% CAGR (2024-2035)
View report →

How to read this

1
This is the TAM (Total Addressable Market) The entire global sports training industry. FullControl doesn't need to capture this whole market — it's the ocean we're swimming in.
2
It's growing steadily at 5.2% per year The underlying market is expanding. More people are paying for organized sports training every year, which means more potential customers for software.
3
This validates the market exists at scale For investors, this number answers "Is this market big enough?" A $27.8B market growing to $50.7B means there's real, growing demand for what these businesses do.
This stat specifically covers the sports training services market (revenue from training), not sports management software (which is the 19.1% CAGR stat). Two different but connected markets.

19.1% — Software CAGR

What this number represents

The Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of the sports management software market. This means the software market is growing ~19% every year, roughly doubling every 4 years.

What is CAGR?

CAGR = (End Value / Start Value)^(1/years) - 1 = ($1.25B / $311M)^(1/8) - 1 = 19.1%

Think of it as the smoothed-out annual growth rate. Even if some years grow faster than others, 19.1% is the average pace.

Where it comes from

Fortune Business Insights Verified
Report: "Sports Management Software Market Size | Industry Report, 2032"
Key finding: Market valued at $311.5M in 2024, projected to reach $1.25B by 2032, at 19.1% CAGR. North America holds 42.6% market share.
View report →
Note: Fortune Business Insights has since updated their report with a newer forecast window ($439.6M in 2026 to $1,777.3M by 2034), but the 19.1% CAGR remains consistent. The original $311M baseline figure is still accurate for the 2024 base year.

Why this matters

1
This is about the software layer, not the training market The $27.8B stat shows demand for training. This stat shows that software for managing these businesses is growing nearly 4x faster than the underlying market.
2
19.1% is exceptionally high For context, the overall SaaS market grows at ~12% CAGR. Sports management software at 19.1% signals a sector that's rapidly digitizing — operators who ran on spreadsheets are buying software.
3
It signals timing High CAGR means the market is still early. Entering a 19% growth market means tailwinds — new customers are appearing faster than competitors can capture them.

$200M+ — SAM Opportunity

What this number represents

The Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) in annual recurring revenue across FullControl's three expansion phases. This is the realistic revenue ceiling if FullControl captured every operator in its target segments.

TAM vs SAM vs SOM

1
TAM = $27.8B (Total Addressable Market) Everyone in the world who does sports training. Way too broad.
2
SAM = $200M+ (Serviceable Addressable Market) Operators in our target geographies (US, Canada, Australia) with 20+ clients, across all three phases. This is who we can actually sell to.
3
SOM = what we actually capture A realistic slice of SAM. Not shown on the investor page — that's what the business plan details.

How $200M+ is derived

The SAM is built bottom-up across three expansion phases:

Phase 1: Basketball Training
3,500 - 4,500 operators (US + Canada + Australia, 20+ clients) x $300/mo = $13-16M ARR
Phase 2: All Sports Training
16,000 - 20,000 operators across all sports x $300/mo = $58-72M ARR
Phase 3: Fitness & Gyms
37,000 - 45,000 independent gyms & fitness businesses (US + Canada) x $300/mo = $133-162M ARR
$13-16M + $58-72M + $133-162M = $204-250M total SAM ARR → "$200M+"

Key assumptions

1
$300/mo blended ARPU Average revenue per user. Blended across plans. This is the assumed price point per operator per month.
2
Operator counts are BAM OS internal estimates These aren't from third-party reports. They're bottom-up estimates based on industry association data, directory scraping, and market knowledge from running BAM Basketball.
3
Same product, broader positioning The core product doesn't change between phases. Basketball trainers and gym owners have the same operational problems. Each phase widens the funnel.
