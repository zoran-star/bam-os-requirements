Why 21 months total runway
We need 18 months to hit the milestone (6 to build + 12 to acquire), plus 3 months of buffer to actually fundraise the seed round. Standard seed fundraises take 2–4 months. Less buffer than that = we're pitching the seed with thin data; more than that = we're over-diluting now.
Section 03 · The milestone

175 users. $43K MRR. That's the bar.

Earlier we toyed with 200–400 users. After pressure-testing what actually makes a strong seed conversation for vertical B2B SaaS, we landed here.

Why 175 and not 300

Seed investors don't score on user count — they score on MRR × quality signals (retention, CAC payback, repeatability). For our category, the thresholds are:

PositionMRR floorUsers @ $250 ARPUWhat it unlocks
Bare minimum$20–30K~100 usersSeed conversation possible, low leverage
Comfortable seed$40–50K~175 usersOur target. Strong conversation, clean terms
Strong position$60K+~240 usersPremium valuation, multiple term sheets
The sweet spot argument
Over-targeting at 300 means raising more, diluting more, and being punished for missing. Under-targeting at 100 means no leverage at the seed. 175 is the sweet spot — defensible without being aggressive. Investors get excited here because we have repeatability + retention data, not because we hit a vanity number.

Why this is defensible to investors
A blended CAC of $293 with 1.6-month payback is exceptional for vertical SaaS. The reason it's not fantasy: 55% of our acquisitions come from BAM warm network or organic content we already produce for BAM Basketball (500K+ YT subs). Paid ads aren't doing the heavy lifting — distribution is.

The riskiest assumption

Section 09 · Investor Q&A prep

What angels will ask. What we say.

Every question an angel asks tests one of our assumptions. Here are the ones we already stress-tested, with our answers.

Why 175 users and not 300?
At $250 ARPU, 175 users = $43K MRR, which sits squarely in "comfortable seed" territory for vertical B2B SaaS. Over-targeting creates downside risk without upside reward: if we raise $700K for 300 users and only hit 200, we're punished. If we raise $550K for 175 users and hit 200, we're celebrated.
Paid CAC at $550 — how do you know?
Honestly, we don't yet. We've benchmarked against vertical SaaS at our ARPU range ($200–300/mo B2B) where CAC typically lands $400–800. We've budgeted at the middle, with a 30% contingency buffer on paid spend. First action post-raise is 90 days of paid testing to validate this assumption.
Why is blended CAC so low at $293?
Because 55% of our acquisition comes from BAM warm network and organic content — channels we already own and operate. Coleman has 500K+ YouTube subs on BAM Basketball, 20+ BAM Business clients, and relationships across 35+ countries. We're not a generalist startup running cold paid acquisition. We're a distribution company extending into SaaS.
What if you only hit 50% of the user target?
100 users × $250 = $25K MRR. That's still bare-minimum seed territory. We'd raise a smaller bridge and reset, or use the remaining runway to double down on whichever channel worked best. The 21-month runway gives us 3 months to pivot and re-raise before running out of cash.
Why angels and not a VC lead?
At pre-seed, for a vertical SaaS that most generalist VCs don't understand, angels give us four things a VC can't: speed (days vs months), right-sized checks ($25–75K vs $500K+), warm intros to the exact operators who'll be customers, and less distraction from building. We plan to bring in a VC lead at seed, once we have the traction to command a priced round.
Why a SAFE at $12M cap?
$12M cap reflects the strength of the founding team (Coleman's 10+ years building BAM, Zoran's technical depth, Rex's CFO-level advisory), the distribution moat, and comparable pre-seed vertical SaaS rounds. The 15% discount is standard. SAFE over priced round because it's cheaper legally, faster to close, and doesn't require us to set a formal valuation this early.
What happens month 21 if you miss?
Three options, in order of preference: (1) raise a small bridge from existing angels at the same cap, (2) take a down-round seed if traction justifies it, (3) slow burn to extend runway 3–6 months and re-attempt fundraise. Our contingency is diversification — Coleman's BAM Business income covers personal overhead, which means FullControl doesn't need to pay founders in a crisis.
