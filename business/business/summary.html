Trainers’ tools today are either too complex, too weak, or both.

§ 03 · Comparables

Nobody covers everything. That’s the gap.

Every competitor owns 1–3 domains. No single platform covers marketing, sales, scheduling, member management, content, payments, and AI for this vertical.

Tier 1 — Direct Competitors

Same vertical, similar ambition. These are the closest comparisons.

CoachIQ
Founded 2019 · 450+ businesses · $50–$150/mo

How they’re similar: Same audience (private sports coaches), same “all-in-one” pitch, same vertical. Scheduling, payments, CRM, website builder, video analysis, branded app. Raised $1.3M pre-seed (Dec 2024).

What’s missing: Essentially a customer-facing scheduling app with a history of moving too slow to adapt to what their clients actually need. Operators still cobble together separate tools for everything beyond booking.
Momence
Founded 2020 · 4,500+ businesses · ~$100+/mo

How they’re similar: “Replace your tool stack” positioning. Scheduling, payments, CRM, marketing, SMS, video, staff management. YC-backed. Grew to 4,500+ businesses in under 5 years.

What’s missing: Built for yoga/wellness studios, not the coach-led, multi-location academy model. No AI that takes action. Marketing is email/SMS blasts, not conversational AI that books trials and handles objections. No content management.

Tier 2 — Overlapping, Different Audience

Validates the market and proves demand, but built for a different customer.

Mindbody
Founded 2000 · 40K+ businesses · $139–$699/mo

How they’re similar: The incumbent. Broadest feature set in the space — scheduling, payments/POS, member management, marketing, staff, payroll. 40K businesses proves massive demand.

What’s missing: Old, expensive, and complex. AI is bolted on — dashboards and “insights,” not a system that acts. Built for studios and gyms, not the mobile coach-led model. Operators still need separate tools for lead gen and content. $139–$699/mo and still requires workarounds.
Exercise.com
Founded 2010 · “Thousands” · $125+/mo

How they’re similar: Similar breadth — gym management, workout programming, payments, scheduling, custom-branded apps. Serves thousands of fitness professionals.

What’s missing: No AI. No marketing automation. No conversational lead nurture. Focused on gym/personal training model, not multi-session academy operations. No content management or business intelligence.

Tier 3 — Market Context

Not direct competitors, but show how fragmented the landscape is and what operators are stitching together today.

GoHighLevel
Founded 2018 · 2M+ users · $97–$497/mo

How they’re similar: All-in-one marketing & sales platform. CRM, funnels, SMS, scheduling, payments, workflow automation. Shows what broad platform adoption looks like at scale (2M+ users, 60K+ agencies).

What’s missing: Horizontal — built for agencies, not operators. No sports-specific features (attendance, sessions, member health, credits/pauses). Requires weeks of setup and an agency or VA to configure ($500–$2K/mo extra). You become a GHL admin instead of a coach.
TeamSnap
Founded 2009 · 25–30M users · Free–$18/mo

How they’re similar: Proves sports software can scale to tens of millions of users. Scheduling, communication, registration, payments, rostering for youth sports leagues and clubs.

What’s missing: Built for recreational team coordination, not business management. No CRM, no marketing, no AI, no business intelligence. Doesn’t help operators grow revenue or retain members.
Acuity Scheduling
Founded 2006 · ~16K+ clients · $16–$49/mo

Booking only. No CRM, marketing, or member management.

TeamReach
Founded 2016 · 4M+ users · Free

Team communication app. Messaging and event RSVPs. No payments, CRM, or marketing.

Playbook
Founded 2016 · 2K+ creators · $15/mo

Content monetization only. Coaches upload workouts, consumers subscribe. Not a business platform.

§ 05 · Go to Market

Start where our network is. Let performance pull us into every sport.

Phase 1 · Own Basketball

We start where we have the most operator relationships. Co-founder Coleman Ayers runs By Any Means Basketball — 500K+ followers, 5 locations, 10K+ athletes. Through By Any Means Business, we’ve worked hands-on with 30+ basketball academies, and Coleman and Mike’s combined network covers 150+ operator contacts across 40+ countries and 100+ cities.

Basketball is our proving ground. Full product depth across every business model an operator runs:

  • Training classes & memberships
  • Team management
  • Gym rental
  • Internal leagues
  • External leagues — under evaluation

Every feature, every AI workflow, every onboarding flow gets stress-tested against real academies first. We’re not cold-starting — we’re productizing relationships that already exist.

Phase 2 · Expand into Sports Performance

This is our second-deepest network, and it’s the smartest bridge into every other sport. Performance facilities (S&C, speed, agility) serve athletes from soccer, football, baseball, volleyball, lacrosse, hockey — all under one roof. Land one performance facility, reach ten sports’ worth of athletes and parents.

Product coverage:

  • Training classes & memberships
  • Gym/facility rental
  • Player feedback & progression tracking

Performance training is also the purest all-memberships business model in sports — no teams, no seasons, no league calendars. The fit is clean and the cross-sport pollination is free.

Phase 3 · Replicate into Other Sports

Once the basketball + performance playbook is proven, we port the same business-model coverage into the next wave — starting where our performance facilities have already introduced FullControl to operators and parents:

  • Soccer
  • Baseball / softball
  • Volleyball
  • Tennis
  • Gymnastics
  • Martial arts

Each new sport is a terminology and AI-prompt configuration on the same engine — not a rebuild. We compound credibility in one vertical before touching the next, and let performance do the cross-sport marketing for free.
