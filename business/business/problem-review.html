Problem Section Review

A critical breakdown of what's in the investor summary problem section today, what's missing, and questions to fill the gaps.

What's there now

Current problem section — 3 sentences

"Trainers run their business on 5–8 disconnected tools"

CRM, scheduling, payments, marketing, analytics, member management — each one solves part of the problem. None of them work together. Operators spend 30–40 hours a week on manual admin instead of coaching. No single platform covers everything an academy actually needs.

This reads like a topic sentence, not a problem section. It names the pain at a surface level but doesn't make an investor feel it, believe it, or understand the financial consequences of it. Below is what's missing.

What's lacking

Missing Who exactly is the customer?

"Trainers" is too broad. An investor needs to picture the person — how big is their business, how many clients, how much revenue, how many staff? Without this, the problem feels abstract. Paint the operator so the pain has a face.

Missing A day-in-the-life moment

The best problem sections make you live the chaos for 10 seconds. What does Monday morning actually look like? What's the first thing they do? What breaks? Right now it's a list of tool categories — not a story. Even one concrete sentence changes everything.

Missing Financial cost of the problem

"30–40 hours a week" is good but not enough. What does that cost in dollars? What are they spending on tool subscriptions? What revenue are they losing from missed follow-ups, churned members, no-shows they never re-engaged? Investors think in dollars.

Missing Evidence / proof the problem is real

The playbook page cites "73% say tech is too complicated" — that stat isn't here. There's no sourcing, no data from your 30+ academy relationships, no quote from an operator. Right now these are claims. One data point or one real quote turns a claim into proof.

Missing What they've tried and why it failed

Operators aren't sitting idle. They've tried GHL, Mindbody, spreadsheets, VAs. What happened? Why didn't it work? Showing failed alternatives proves the problem is unsolved, not just unaddressed. The comparables section covers this later, but the problem section should plant the seed.

Weak Specific workflows that break

"None of them work together" is the right point, but it's vague. Which specific workflow breaks? A lead comes in from Instagram → nobody follows up for 3 days → lead goes cold. A member misses 2 weeks → nobody notices until they cancel. Give one or two concrete breakdowns.

Missing Second-order consequences

The first-order problem is "too many tools, too much admin." But what does that cause? Members churn because nobody flagged them. Revenue plateaus because the owner is buried in ops instead of growing. Coaches burn out and quit. The second-order effects are what make the problem feel urgent.

Weak Why is this getting worse?

There's no sense of urgency or trend. Are there more tools now than 5 years ago? Are operators' expectations rising because of AI in other industries? Is competition between academies increasing? A growing problem is more investable than a static one.
